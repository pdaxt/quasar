What is Quantum Computing?

The One-Sentence Summary: Quantum computers use the weird rules of quantum physics to solve certain problems exponentially faster than regular computers.

Classical vs Quantum: The Key Difference

Classical Computer Quantum Computer
Bits are either 0 OR 1 Qubits can be 0 AND 1 simultaneously
Processes one possibility at a time Processes many possibilities at once
Bits are independent Qubits can be "entangled" (connected)
Reading data is non-destructive Measuring destroys the quantum state

Understanding Qubits

What is a Qubit?

A qubit (quantum bit) is the fundamental unit of quantum information. Unlike a classical bit that's either 0 or 1, a qubit can be in a superposition of both states.

Analogy: Think of a coin. A classical bit is like a coin lying on the table - it's either heads (0) or tails (1). A qubit is like a coin spinning in the air - it's both heads AND tails until it lands (is measured).

Quantum Gates

Quantum gates are operations that transform qubit states. They're like the logic gates (AND, OR, NOT) of classical computing, but for quantum states.

Key Property: All quantum gates are reversible. You can always undo a gate by applying its inverse. This is required by quantum mechanics!

Entanglement: The "Spooky" Part

Einstein called it "spooky action at a distance" - and it's real!

What is Entanglement?

When qubits are entangled, measuring one instantly affects what you'll measure on the other, no matter how far apart they are.

The Bell State Explained

|Bell‚ü© = (|00‚ü© + |11‚ü©) / ‚àö2

This state says:

  • 50% chance both qubits are 0
  • 50% chance both qubits are 1
  • 0% chance they're different!

The Weird Part: Before measurement, neither qubit has a definite value. But the moment you measure one, the other "knows" what to be - instantly, even if it's on the other side of the universe!

GHZ State: 3-Way Entanglement

|GHZ‚ü© = (|000‚ü© + |111‚ü©) / ‚àö2

Three qubits, all entangled. Measure any one, and you know what the other two will be.
